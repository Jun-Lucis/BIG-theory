# Limitations and Scope of Boundary Information Geometry (BIG)

This document summarizes the intended scope and limitations of **Boundary Information Geometry (BIG)**.

BIG is a developing boundary-centered research programme. It is not presented as a completed physical theory or as a quantitatively calibrated model of any specific physical, biological, cognitive, or technological system.

---

## 1. General status

BIG should currently be understood as a collection of:

* conceptual principles,
* reduced mathematical models,
* numerical experiments,
* structural comparisons,
* and exploratory extensions.

These layers do not have the same evidential status.

A numerical result in a reduced model should not be read as a direct prediction about a real physical system unless the relevant domain-specific equations, parameters, units, calibration, and validation have been added.

---

## 2. What BIG currently claims

The current BIG programme supports limited, model-level claims such as:

* boundary layers can behave as active structures rather than passive edges;
* local boundary profiles can exhibit robust quadratic-like landing in certain reduced PDE models;
* boundary anisotropy can shift finite-time survival/runaway thresholds in a specified numerical model;
* boundary cost versus nonlocal repulsion can generate a fission-like metastable landscape in a reduced geometric model;
* finite noise can enable and then destabilize sustained capture in a reduced dynamic boundary model;
* post-capture hidden-depth dynamics can preserve multiple parent memories in a stochastic reduced model;
* boundary approach, resonance locking, and hidden-depth inheritance can coexist in a unified reduced B12 model.

These are structural and numerical claims within specified models.

---

## 3. What BIG does not currently claim

BIG does **not** currently claim to be:

* a completed theory of fundamental physics;
* a replacement for general relativity, quantum mechanics, quantum field theory, nuclear physics, or statistical mechanics;
* a quantitative model of nuclear fission;
* a quantitative model of nuclear fusion;
* a quantitative theory of biological inheritance;
* a quantitative model of consciousness or subjective qualia;
* a calibrated theory of cosmology or dark matter;
* a validated engineering model for materials, membranes, or interface failure.

Some BIG models may show structural analogies with these fields, but such analogies are not the same as calibrated physical equivalence.

---

## 4. B9 scope

BIG-B9 studies a minimal energy competition,

$$
E(\Omega;\lambda)=\sigma P(\Omega)+\lambda C(\Omega),
$$

where (P(\Omega)) is a boundary cost and (C(\Omega)) is a Coulomb-like nonlocal repulsion term.

The model can generate a fission-like metastable energy landscape.

However, BIG-B9 is **not** a quantitative model of nuclear fission. It does not include shell corrections, pairing effects, quantum tunneling, excitation-energy dependence, fragment yields, cross-section normalization, or calibration to nuclear observables.

The comparison with nuclear fission should be read as a structural and macroscopic analogy.

---

## 5. B10 scope

BIG-B10 studies finite-noise sustained capture in a reduced dynamic boundary model.

It is not a quantitative model of nuclear fusion. It does not model Coulomb-barrier penetration, quantum tunneling, nuclear potentials, reaction cross sections, plasma kinetics, or real fusion energy release.

The term “fusion-like” refers to a structural sequence of approach, activation, capture, and sustained locking in the reduced model.

---

## 6. B11 scope

BIG-B11 studies post-capture hidden-depth inheritance versus assimilation in a stochastic reduced landscape.

It is not a quantitative theory of biological inheritance, nuclear fusion, or real energy release.

Energy drops reported in B11 are model-defined effective decreases in the post-fusion hidden-depth potential landscape.

---

## 7. B12 scope

BIG-B12 connects B9, B10, and B11 into a reduced unified model with three sectors:

* boundary distance,
* internal resonance,
* hidden-depth inheritance.

The main result is a model-level finite-noise R-lock window with post-lock inheritance.

BIG-B12 is not a completed physical unification theory. It is a reduced variational-stochastic model showing that boundary approach, noise-assisted resonance locking, and hidden-depth inheritance can coexist within one mathematical framework.

---

## 8. Structural comparison versus quantitative prediction

A recurring theme in BIG is that reduced boundary models sometimes resemble known structures in other fields.

For example:

* B9 resembles surface-versus-repulsion competition in macroscopic fission intuition;
* B10 resembles stochastic-resonance-like capture;
* B11 resembles inheritance-versus-assimilation competition;
* B8 resembles geometry-controlled finite-time threshold behavior.

These should be described as **structural correspondences** or **model-level analogies**, not as quantitative predictions.

---

## 9. Future adaptation

BIG may be useful as a boundary-centered language for exploring other domains where the following are central:

* interface stability,
* free-boundary dynamics,
* finite-amplitude thresholds,
* anisotropy,
* separation,
* capture,
* memory retention,
* non-assimilative interaction,
* and structural persistence.

However, each domain requires its own equations, parameters, validation standards, and comparison with existing literature.

---

## 10. Recommended cautious wording

When describing BIG publicly, the following wording is recommended:

> Boundary Information Geometry is a boundary-centered research programme exploring how individuality, stability, separation, capture, and inheritance may arise in reduced mathematical and numerical models. Its current results are structural and model-level, not calibrated physical predictions.

For B9:

> BIG-B9 provides a structural comparison with fission-like metastability, not a quantitative nuclear-fission model.

For B10:

> BIG-B10 studies stochastic-resonance-like finite-noise capture in a reduced dynamic boundary model, not physical nuclear fusion.

For B11:

> BIG-B11 studies non-assimilative hidden-depth inheritance in a stochastic reduced model, not biological inheritance or real energy release.

For B12:

> BIG-B12 shows that boundary approach, finite-noise resonance locking, and hidden-depth inheritance can coexist in a unified reduced model.
